# 🛒 Product & Price Pulse – E-Commerce Analytics Dashboard

Power BI dashboard analyzing pricing, discounts, inventory, ratings, and customer engagement across 1,000 products and 5 e-commerce platforms to uncover actionable pricing and product performance insights.

## 📌 Overview
Product & Price Pulse is an end-to-end Power BI analytics project designed to analyze product pricing, discounts, inventory availability, ratings, and brand performance across multiple e-commerce platforms.

The dashboard combines product, pricing, and customer engagement metrics into a single reporting solution, helping stakeholders identify pricing opportunities, monitor discount strategies, compare platform performance, and track product availability.

## 📌 Business Problem

If you sell—or shop for—the same products across multiple e-commerce platforms, prices, discounts, stock availability, and customer engagement can vary significantly. Keeping track of these differences manually is time-consuming and often leads to missed opportunities.

For businesses, this can mean:

- Missing opportunities to price products competitively
- Losing potential sales due to stockouts
- Difficulty evaluating whether discounts are driving customer engagement
- Limited visibility into which platforms, brands, or categories are performing best

Without a centralized view, identifying pricing trends, monitoring inventory health, and comparing platform performance becomes challenging. 

#### This project was created to bring those insights together in a single dashboard and make product performance easier to track and analyze.


## 🎯 Business Objective
Online retailers operate across multiple platforms with varying pricing strategies, discount levels, inventory positions, and customer engagement patterns.

The objective of this project was to build a centralized dashboard that allows users to:

- Compare product pricing across retail platforms
- Analyze discount distribution and effectiveness
- Monitor inventory availability
- Evaluate brand and category performance
- Identify highly rated and highly engaged products
- Support data-driven pricing and merchandising decisions

## 💡 Solution

I gathered, cleaned, modeled and built an interactive Power BI dashboard using data from 1,000+ products spread evenly across five retail platforms — Amazon, Noon, AliExpress, Jumia, and eBay (200 products each).
The dashboard brings pricing, discounts, inventory availability, ratings, and customer engagement into a single view, making it easier to compare platform performance and identify opportunities.

### Key metrics include:

- Discount penetration rate — (28.4%) of products are currently discounted
- In-stock availability — (83.4%) of products are in stock at any given time
- Average pricing across platforms and categories
- Customer engagement score — a custom measure combining review volume and rating score, to surface products that are both trusted and popular
- Lowest price per product — a DAX measure that automatically highlights the platform offering the best price for each product, so there's no manual comparison needed


### ⚙️ Technical Highlights

Created custom DAX measures to calculate discount penetration, customer engagement, and automatically identify the lowest-priced platform for each product. Conditional formatting was used to highlight the best-priced option, allowing users to compare prices instantly without manual analysis.

## 🔍 Key Insights

### Pricing

- Jumia recorded the highest average product prices among all platforms.
- AliExpress consistently offered the lowest average prices.
- Product pricing varied significantly across platforms, highlighting potential arbitrage and pricing optimization opportunities.

### Inventory

- Over 83% of products were available in stock.
- Stock availability remained relatively consistent across all platforms.

### Discounts

- Only 28.4% of products were discounted.
- Noon recorded the highest number of discounted products.
- Most products were sold without promotional discounts.

### Brands & Customer Engagement

- The brand "New" achieved the highest average rating of 4.25.
- Beauty products represented the largest product category.
- Customer engagement varied significantly across products, indicating opportunities for targeted promotions.

## ✅ Recommendations

#### Based on what the data surfaced:

1. Standardize price monitoring across platforms
Product prices vary across platforms, creating opportunities to adjust pricing and remain competitive in the market.

2. Use discounts more strategically
With only 28.4% of products currently discounted, targeted promotions could help improve visibility and sales in underperforming categories.

3. Reduce stockouts in high-demand categories
While 83.4% of products are in stock, improving availability in popular categories can help prevent missed sales opportunities.

4. Promote high-engagement products
Products with strong ratings and review activity already have customer trust and are good candidates for marketing campaigns and promotional offers.

5. Support top-performing brands and platforms
Brands and platforms with strong customer engagement and ratings should receive additional focus, while lower-performing areas should be reviewed for improvement opportunities.
